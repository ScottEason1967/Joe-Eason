The Complete 8

What Makes a Complete Central Midfielder?

A central midfielder who genuinely does a bit of everything is rare. Here's the evidence that Joe Eason is one.

The number 8 role demands more than any other position on the pitch. You need to defend, carry, create, tackle, head, pass short, pass long, read the game and set the tempo. Most players do two or three of those well. Joe does all of them, and the footage, data and scout assessments from the 2025/26 season back it up.

Below, each area of his game is broken down with video evidence from senior men's football at Step 5, supported by verified athletic testing data and third-party assessments. This isn't academy football against kids his own age. This is grown men's football, and he's been playing it regularly since he was 16.

Ball Carrying & Driving Runs

Joe carries the ball with purpose. He doesn't dribble for the sake of it — he drives forward to break lines, commit defenders and create advantages for the players around him. At 6'2" and 84kg with a verified top speed of 34 km/h, defenders can't just bump him off the ball or run him down.

Watch how he picks his moments: he recognises when a carry is more effective than a pass, and he has the physical tools to execute it against senior men.

Technical Quality & Close Control

Two-footed, composed on the ball, and clean in his execution. Joe's first touch is consistently good under pressure, and he picks the right pass rather than the safe one. His weight of pass — the ability to play the ball at exactly the right pace — is something scouts have highlighted repeatedly.

"Superb two-footed technician with vision and awareness. Dominates through intelligence and calmness in possession." Mark Hodgson, Head Coach, Brazilian Soccer Schools

03

Receiving & Turning Under Pressure

This is the bit that separates midfielders who can play at a higher level from those who can't. Can you receive the ball with your back to goal, with a man on you, and play forward? Joe does it consistently. He receives on his back foot, uses his body to shield, and turns or plays forward before the pressure arrives.

"Receive on back foot well, hold off man, head up to know where to play early or utilises his strength to roll man and play forward." David Banjura, Former National League Chief Scout

04

Short Passing & Tempo Control

Joe dictates the rhythm of the game. When the team needs to keep it, he keeps it. When the tempo needs to go up, he accelerates it. His short passing is crisp and purposeful — he doesn't just recycle; he moves the team forward through intelligent combinations.

"Powerful runner, good range of passing and able to control the tempo of the game. Comfortable in possession in any area of the park." William Kinniburgh, Scout, Motherwell / Oxford United

05

Longer Range Passing, Switches & Crossing

This is where Joe's range really shows. He switches the play accurately over 40-50 yards, fizzes balls through the lines into feet and runners, and delivers quality from wide and deep positions. The ability to see a forward pass and execute it with the right weight is what multiple scouts have flagged as his standout quality.

"Your ability to see a forward pass is excellent. Your control and composure is very good. The standard in your passing and receiving a ball in space would only improve with better players around you." William Kinniburgh, Scout, Motherwell / Oxford United

Out of Possession: Blocking, Tackling & Defensive Cover

A midfielder who can't defend is half a player. Joe wins the ball cleanly, blocks shots, and covers his centre-backs when they step forward. He reads the game early enough to be in position before the danger develops. He's not just willing to defend — he's good at it.

Recovery Runs, Interceptions & Heading

At 6'2" with elite sprint acceleration (0.91s over 5m), Joe wins aerial duels and recovers ground quickly when caught upfield. The Numbers Behind It

Marske concede 0.91 goals per 90 minutes with Joe on the pitch, compared to 2.74 without him. That's based on 987 minutes on pitch (10 goals conceded) versus 1,443 minutes without (44 goals conceded). The pattern is consistent across a meaningful sample.

This isn't a coincidence or a small-sample anomaly. It's the result of his positioning intelligence, communication, willingness to cover, and ability to read transitions early. He organises the team around him from minute one.
